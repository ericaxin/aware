ABOUT

AWARE offers early Alzheimer's patients autonomy and freedom with safety in mind and allows intergenerational trust building through its DIY assembly process. The system consists of two paired devices connected by bluetooth. A prerecorded message is played when the connection is broken.

I. Building Trust

Modern technology can sometimes seem foreign and intimidating to older generations. AWARE offers an opportunity for different generations to sit together and assemble the device in collaboration. The process can spark conversations and allow both parties to reach a mutual understanding of each other’s intentions.
AWARE serves as a mechanism to create bonds and build trust between generations. It is a token of care that can be passed down from generation to generation.

II. Giving Care

According to the United Nations, 50 million people are believed to be living with Alzheimer's disease or other dementias. One in ten people (10%) age 65 and older has Alzheimer's disease. A new case of Alzheimer’s is diagnosed every 70 seconds in the US. While family members pay lots of attention to the safety of Alzheimer’s patients, psychological needs are often neglected. Alzheimer’s patients can feel frustrated or controlled if they are forced to stay home.
AWARE is a caregiving device that reduces unnecessary physical constraints.

III. Ensuring Safety

Alzheimer’s patients often remember some people and events while forgetting others. A familiar and recognized voice can comfort Alzheimer's patients, and help them better understand and respond.
AWARE ensures safety of the user by playing messages or reminders when the user is out of a predetermined range. Maximum range possible is 100m or 328ft. Family members will be notified when signal is lost for over 5 minutes.

ASSEMBLY
Device A

Arduino Nano | Bluetooth Module | Battery
• connected with device B through bluetooth
• sends periodic heartbeat signal to device B

Device B

Arduino Nano | Bluetooth Module | SD Card Module | Speaker | LED Lights | Battery
• expects to receive the signal continuously
• if no signals are received for over two periods, an alarm will be triggered
alarm can be customized (message, reminder, etc.)

Estimated Time Required: 1 hour

I. Hardware

Connecting SD Card Module to Arduino

• connect 5V pin to 5V pin
• connect GND pin to GND pin
• connect CLK pin to pin 13
• connect DO pin to pin 12
• connect DI pin to pin 11
• connect CS pin to pin 10

Connecting Bluetooth Module to Arduino

• connect RXD to D7
• connect TXD to D6
• connect Key to D5
• connect VCC to 5V
• connect GND to GND

Connecting Speaker to Arduino

• connect pin 9 to GND

Connecting LED Lights to Arduino

• connect positive side of green LED to resistor then pin 3
• connect positive side of red LED to resistor then pin 4
• connect both negative sides to GND

Connecting Battery to Arduino

• connect black wire to GND
• connect red wire to VIN
